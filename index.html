Introduction to Climate Change

Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time.While climate change is a natural phenomenon, scientific evidence shows that human activities,particularly the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, have accelerated these changes. This has led to an increase in greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, causing global temperatures to rise.

Causes of Climate Change

The primary cause of recent climate change is the increase in greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O). These gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, creating a "greenhouse effect." Major sources of these emissions include burning fossil fuels for energy, deforestation, industrial activities, and agriculture.

Effects of Climate Change

Climate change has far-reaching effects on the environment and human societies. Rising temperatures lead to more extreme weather events like hurricanes, heatwaves, and floods. Melting ice caps and glaciers contribute to sea level rise, threatening coastal communities. Changes in weather patterns can disrupt ecosystems, agriculture, and water supplies, leading to food and water shortages.
